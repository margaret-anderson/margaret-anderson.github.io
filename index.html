About Me

I am a Boston-based graduate student with a dual interest in physics and medieval history. Though my formal field of study is condensed matter physics, I love developing ways to use physics experimental techniques to learn more about historical artifacts and manuscripts. Imaging and microscopy methods provide a unique perspective of materials, epitaxially-grown crystals and medieval manuscripts alike.

I am starting the first year of graduate school virtually (thanks, COVID), but I will spend this year exploring my research interests, finding a suitable mentor, and shoring up my physics knowledge in classes. Outside of school, I love baking, crafting, watching television, and learning new skills (like web development).
